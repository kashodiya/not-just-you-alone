## Summary
These notes develop a critical counterweight to the project's existing systemic narrative. Core argument: systemic explanations are correct but insufficient. Mental strength has no institutional home, no institution is coming to replace the ones that failed, and the individual is the only leverage point where the cycle can break — not because it's fair, but because it's true.

## Tonal Framework for All New Content

Every new entity follows a three-part structure:
1. **Acknowledge** — Yes, the system failed you. Here's how. (Compassion)
2. **Name the limit** — Understanding alone won't change your inner experience. (Honest, not shaming)
3. **Offer the path** — Here's what you can do, starting small. (Warm, practical)

Energy: **physiotherapist after an injury** — not drill sergeant, not "just rest."
- "Nobody taught you this. That's real. But you can teach yourself."
- "A muscle never exercised isn't weak from laziness — it was never used. It can still grow."
- Never moralize. Never "toughen up." Never "just push through."

| 1 | `insight-knowledge-not-strength` | Knowing Why You're Drowning Doesn't Teach You to Swim | Intellectual understanding of a problem is not the inner power to overcome it. The smoking doctor knows everything and still smokes. Knowledge is the map, not the muscle. |

### ADDING — New Actions (4)

| # | ID | Title | Core Idea |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 | `action-promise-keeping` | Make small promises and keep them | Foundation of self-trust. Not grand resolutions — daily micro-commitments honored regardless of mood. Scale matters less than consistency. |
| 4 | `action-voluntary-discomfort` | Choose difficulty as training | Cold exposure, fasting, silence, boredom — deliberately chosen. Physical/sensory training as entry point. Differs from existing `action-discomfort-tolerance` which focuses on emotional/relational discomfort. |
| 5 | `action-find-mentor` | Find one real person who embodies strength | Not an influencer. A real person whose life demonstrates what they teach. One relationship can transmit what no institution can. Assumes zero institutional support — this works even if family, school, religion are absent. |
| 6 | `action-physical-discipline` | Use the body as entry point to self-governance | Exercise, food, sleep — not for appearance but as daily practice of acting against impulse. The body is concrete, measurable, and immediately honest. Easiest domain to start building self-mastery. |

| 7 | `pattern-diagnosis-without-cure` | The Understanding Trap | Understand problem → feel validated → no inner change → still stuck → seek more understanding. Sophistication increases, strength doesn't. |
